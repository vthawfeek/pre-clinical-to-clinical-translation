I got 100% accuracy on my cancer cell line to patient alignment model. Then I spent two weeks trying
to prove myself wrong.

[attach permutation_null.png]

That figure is the one negative control I trust most. It shuffles which cell-line cancer type
corresponds to which patient cancer type, breaking the real correspondence the model's loss depends
on, then asks it to solve the same retrieval task on nonsense labels. Across 100 shuffled runs, the
best the model could ever do was 17-20%, and the average sat at 7.0-7.7%, right where random chance
should be for a 15-lineage problem. My real result: 78.4%. Empirical p = 0.0099. When there is
nothing real to find, it correctly finds nothing.

That was the last of seven adversarial checks I ran against my own 100% test result:

→ Multi-seed stability: 10 independent re-splits, mean kNN@5 95.0% ± 3.4%, CI [93.2%, 97.1%]. Not a
  lucky 38-line test set.
→ Real baselines: beats Harmony batch correction (84.2%) by 15.8 points. But a plain supervised
  classifier with zero cross-domain alignment already hits 97.1% on the same task. My model's real
  edge is 2.9 points. Uncomfortable, and the number I trust most because I went looking for it.
→ Scaled to 15 lineages, including lung/colorectal/glioma pairs that are genuinely hard to tell
  apart: accuracy drops to 78.4% (expected), and 45.8% of the errors land on the biologically
  related pairs despite those being only 3.8% of possible mistakes. When it fails, it fails toward
  real biology.
→ Tumour purity confounder: ruled out. Lineage retrieval holds at 100% in both high- and
  low-purity patient strata.
→ Celligner head-to-head: attempted, blocked by an unresolvable dependency in the published
  package. Reported as a gap, not hidden.
→ Vemurafenib case study: BRAF-mutant melanoma cell lines sit weakly-but-really closer to
  BRAF-mutant patients (p = 0.047). Whether that proximity predicts actual drug sensitivity: null
  (p = 0.19, n = 41). I checked whether the embedding threw away that signal versus raw gene
  expression. Neither representation could recover it at this sample size. Inconclusive, not a
  clean rescue.

None of this is a clinical claim. It has not touched an external cohort, a different sequencing
platform, or anything prospective, and it is not meant to. What it is: a released, reproducible
harness for stress-testing exactly this class of result before treating a clean number as biological
insight.
