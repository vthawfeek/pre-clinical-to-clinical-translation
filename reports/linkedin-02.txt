Two weeks ago: cancer cell lines and human tumours formed completely separate clouds
in gene expression space.

Today: [attach umap_before_after.png]

Left: raw PCA. CCLE cell lines and TCGA patients sit in two separate clouds. The
biggest axis of variation is not lung vs breast vs skin, it is dish vs body. That is
the clinical translation problem in mathematical form.

Right: the trained dual-tower model's UMAP. The two clouds are gone. LUAD cell lines
cluster with LUAD patients. BRCA with BRCA. SKCM with SKCM. Each cell line lands
inside its own lineage's patient cloud.

kNN@5 retrieval accuracy on the held-out test set: 100% (random baseline 33.3%,
PCA+kNN baseline 65.8%, Harmony reference ~63%). Honest caveat: the test set is small,
38 cell lines against 339 patients, so read 100% as a strong signal, not a benchmark.
kNN@1 is 97.4%, a single miss.

That miss is the interesting part. It is Calu-6, an anaplastic lung line that has lost
its differentiation markers, so the model correctly flags it as a weak stand-in for a
typical lung adenocarcinoma patient. Every cell line now carries a Translational
Fidelity Score, and the four lowest breast lines are all triple-negative, which is the
model telling the truth about a label problem: BRCA is really four molecular subtypes
collapsed into one bucket.
